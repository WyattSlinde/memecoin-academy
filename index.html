01 · The Basics

What is a memecoin?

A memecoin is a cryptocurrency with no product, no company, and no promised utility — its entire value comes from attention, community, and speculation. Think of it as a tradable internet joke or movement. Dogecoin started it; today thousands launch per day.

How is it different from "real" crypto?

  • Bitcoin / Ethereum: trying to be money or a computing platform. Years of development, huge networks.
  • Utility tokens: power a specific app (e.g., a DEX or game).
  • Memecoins: the value is the meme. Price is pure supply & demand driven by hype. No roadmap required.

Why do people trade them?

  • Tiny starting market caps mean a coin can 10× or 100× fast (and -99% just as fast).
  • Launches are permissionless — anyone can make one in 30 seconds for a couple dollars.
  • It's 24/7, global, and the barrier to entry is basically a wallet.
Mental model A memecoin's price is just how much the last person was willing to pay. There's no underlying cash flow to "value." You're betting on whether more attention shows up after you buy — and getting out before it leaves.
02 · Foundations

The plumbing: Solana, wallets, DEXs & liquidity

Almost all memecoin action lives on Solana because it's fast and dirt-cheap (fractions of a cent per transaction). Before you can trade, four concepts have to click.

A

Blockchain & Solana

A shared public ledger. Every trade is a transaction recorded in a block. Solana produces blocks ~every 400ms, which is why memecoin trading feels near-instant.

B

Wallets & keys

A wallet (Phantom, Solflare, or Axiom's built-in one) holds your SOL and tokens. Your seed phrase = the master password. Anyone with it owns your funds. Never share it. Ever.

C

DEX & liquidity pools

A DEX (decentralized exchange) like Raydium lets you swap tokens with no middleman. You trade against a liquidity pool — a shared pot of two tokens (e.g., SOL + MEME).

D

Why liquidity matters

More liquidity = more stable price & easier exits. Thin liquidity means your own buy/sell swings the price hard (high slippage) — and you may not be able to sell at all.

The AMM in one sentence Most DEXs use an Automated Market Maker: price is set by a formula on the ratio of the two tokens in the pool. Buy MEME with SOL → pool gets more SOL, less MEME → MEME's price goes up. That same math drives the bonding curve you'll meet next.
03 · Birth of a Coin

How a memecoin is born: pump.fun & the bonding curve

Pump.fun is the launchpad where most memecoins are created. It removes every barrier: pay a tiny fee, name your coin, and it's live and tradable in seconds. The magic — and the trap — is the bonding curve.

What's a bonding curve?

A hard-coded formula that sets the price automatically. There's no order book and no seller you negotiate with. The rule is simple:

  • Every buy pushes the price up the curve.
  • Every sell pushes it down.
  • Early buyers pay the least; late buyers pay more.

When enough has been bought to reach roughly a $69,000 market cap, the coin "graduates": ~$12k of liquidity is deposited into a real DEX (PumpSwap or Raydium) and it trades freely from there.

Why "graduation" is a big deal

Before graduation, you're trapped inside the curve. After, the coin has a real liquidity pool and can theoretically run much higher — or get dumped. Graduation is the single most-watched milestone in memecoin trading.

Reality: only ~1.2% of launches ever graduate. The other 98.8% fade to zero, often within minutes.

04 · The Lifecycle

The life of a coin: from mint to migration (or zero)

Every coin moves through stages. Axiom's interface is literally built around three of them. Here's the full journey.

1 · Mint / New

Coin is created on pump.fun. Seconds old. Maximum risk & maximum potential. Snipers buy here. 90%+ die within minutes.

2 · Bonding (Final Stretch)

Buying continues up the curve. As it nears the $69k graduation line, it enters the "Final Stretch" — a key window traders watch.

3 · Graduation / Migrated

Hits ~$69k mcap → liquidity migrates to a real DEX. Now a "mature" coin with a real pool. Often a volatility spike here.

4 · Discovery or Death

Either a community forms and it trends (rare), or volume dries up and it bleeds to zero (common). Most coins end here.

Map this to the terminal Axiom's Pulse feed has three columns that mirror stages 1–3: New Pairs → Final Stretch → Migrated. Once you understand the lifecycle, the interface stops looking like noise. That's chapter 06.

08 · Execution

Placing a trade: slippage, fees & order types

A memecoin trade is a swap (SOL → token, or token → SOL). The settings around it decide whether your trade lands, at what price, and how much you bleed to bots. Get these right before you click.

Slippage

The max price change you'll accept between clicking and executing. Too low → trade fails on a fast-moving coin. Too high → bots/sandwiches eat you. New volatile coins often need higher slippage; established ones can run tight.

Priority fee

A tip to validators to process your transaction sooner. In a race to snipe a launch, a higher priority fee = you land earlier. Costs more, but a failed snipe costs the whole opportunity.

Bribe / Jito tip

An extra payment to get your transaction included in a Jito bundle — the fastest lane on Solana. Used by snipers to win the first block. Overkill for casual trades.

MEV protection

Settings that route your trade to avoid being sandwich-attacked by bots. Worth enabling — bots front-run and back-run unprotected trades to skim from you.

Order types you'll see in Axiom

Quick Buy / Market

Buy/sell instantly at the current price. Fastest, but you accept whatever price slippage gives you. The default for aping.

Limit order

Rests on-chain and only fires at a price you set. Great for "buy if it dips to X" or "sell if it hits Y" without watching the screen.

Ladder / migration sniper

Ladder = split entries/exits across prices (DCA). Migration sniper = auto-buy the instant a coin graduates to the DEX.

Always do this Place a tiny test trade on a new coin before sizing up. Confirm you can sell (some scam tokens let you buy but not sell — a "honeypot"). If you can't exit a small bag, you definitely can't exit a big one.

10 · Staying Alive

Risk management & position sizing

Risking 2% per trade, even 5 losses in a row barely dents you. Now slide risk up to 20% and watch how fast a normal losing streak wipes you out — that's how most people blow up.

Have an exit before you enter

Decide your take-profit (e.g., sell half at 2×) and your stop (e.g., out at -50%) before buying. Emotion in the moment makes terrible decisions.

Take profits on the way up

"Get your initial back" — sell enough at 2× to recover your stake. Now you're playing with house money and can't lose. Most blow-ups come from greed, not bad picks.

Expect to be wrong a lot

In a world where 98% of coins die, most of your trades will lose. The model that works: many small losses + a few big wins > total. Size so the losses don't matter.

The blow-up pattern: win a few, feel invincible, over-size, marry a bag, refuse to sell on the way down, "average down" into a dying coin, account → zero. Recognize it because you will feel it. The rules above exist specifically to stop it.
11 · Advanced

Advanced mechanics: MEV, bundles, snipers & perps

You don't need these to start, but you'll hear the words constantly — and knowing them tells you who you're really trading against.

MEV & sandwich attacks

Bots watch pending trades and insert their own around yours: buy right before you (pushing price up), let your buy push it higher, then sell into you. You overpay; they pocket the difference. MEV protection routes around this.

Jito bundles

A way to submit a group of transactions that land together, in order, in the same block — with a tip to the validator. Snipers use bundles to guarantee they're first. Axiom uses them for fast, protected execution.

Snipers & bots

Automated programs that buy a coin in its first block(s), before humans can react. A sniper bot is often already in profit by the time the launch hits your feed. Assume they exist on every coin.

Copy trading & wallet tracking

Follow a profitable wallet and mirror its trades automatically, or just watch "smart money" wallets for ideas. Useful — but you're always a step behind the wallet you copy, and good wallets know they're watched.

Perps (Hyperliquid)

Perpetual futures let you trade with leverage (borrowed size) and bet on price going down (short). Axiom integrates Hyperliquid for this. Leverage multiplies gains and losses — and can liquidate (wipe) your position. Advanced users only.

Yield on idle SOL

Axiom lets you earn yield/stake SOL that isn't actively trading. Small bonus, but understand any lockup or smart-contract risk before parking funds.

The most important thing on this page

Why you can't just copy them

When researchers simulated copying Cented's trades with just a 10-second delay — faster than any human could react — the follower's return was -21.3%. Read that again. Mirroring a profitable pro, slightly late, lost money.

  • Speed is the edge. By the time a snipe appears on a leaderboard, the easy profit is already extracted. You're buying their exit.
  • Survivorship bias. You see the 5 winners with $10M. You never see the 50,000 wallets that did the exact same thing and went to zero. Their absence isn't proof the strategy works — it's the graveyard you're not shown.
  • Different game. Pros run bots, multiple wallets, bundles, and colocated infrastructure. Hand-trading their style is bringing a knife to a drone fight.
  • Even the community is split. Many call high-frequency snipers "the cancer of memecoins" for extracting value from newcomers rather than building anything. You'd be the newcomer.

Use the pros for principles (discipline, staged exits, strict filters, cut losers fast) — never as trades to blindly mirror. The transferable part is the mindset, not the clicks.

The transferable wisdom (steal these, not the trades)

Sell in stages

Every disciplined pro scales out: recover your initial early, take profit in chunks, let a small "moonbag" ride. Nobody consistently sells the exact top.

Cut losers instantly

The wallets that survive don't "average down" on dying coins. A small loss taken fast beats a bag held to zero. Ego is the most expensive position.

Have a written filter

Locked liquidity, sane holder distribution, rising volume, real community. If a coin fails your checklist, you skip it — no exceptions, no FOMO.

13 · Defense

Scams & how not to get rekt

The fastest way to lose everything isn't a bad trade — it's a scam or a security mistake. Internalize these.

RUG

Rug pull

Dev removes the liquidity (or dumps their bag) and the price goes to zero instantly. Defense: liquidity locked/burned, low dev holdings, revoked mint.

TRAP

Honeypot

You can buy but the contract blocks selling. Your bag is trapped at "all-time high" forever. Defense: tiny test trade — confirm you can sell.

FAKE

Phishing sites

Clone of axiom.trade that steals your seed phrase or drains your wallet on connect. Defense: bookmark the real URL; never type your seed into a website.

PUMP

Pump & dump groups

"Alpha" callers buy first, tell you to buy (you're the exit), then dump on you. Defense: if someone's screaming at you to buy, you're probably the liquidity.

DRAIN

Malicious approvals

Signing a sketchy transaction can grant a contract permission to drain your tokens. Defense: read what you sign; revoke old approvals; use a burner.

IMP

Impersonation

Fake "support", fake devs in DMs, fake giveaways. Real support never DMs you first or asks for your seed. Defense: assume every unsolicited DM is a scam.

Your security baseline (non-negotiable)

  • Use a dedicated burner wallet for memecoins — never your main holdings.
  • Write your seed phrase on paper, offline. Never digital, never shared.
  • Bookmark the real axiom.trade; ignore ads & DM links.
  • Tiny test deposit + tiny test trade before any real size.
  • Enable MEV protection; sanity-check slippage every time.
  • Periodically revoke old token approvals.
  • If a deal feels urgent or too good — that's the scam working.
  • Never invest borrowed money or rent. Only true risk capital.
